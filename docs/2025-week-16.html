The Flamboyant Commish Doused, Championship Dreams Extinguished

Arizona Queer delivered a commanding 29.06-point defeat to The Flamboyant Commish, effectively ending their championship aspirations and ushering them into an early offseason.
By Vince Malloy · Beat Writer, The Hetero Herald

The Hetero Heroes playoffs, a crucible designed to expose the weak, did its job this week, extinguishing The Flamboyant Commish's championship dreams in a rather definitive fashion. Arizona Queer, flexing an undeniable late-season surge, posted a commanding 156.16 points against the Commish’s anemic 127.1, securing a 29.06-point victory that frankly left little to the imagination regarding who showed up to play.

Another year, another semifinal exit for a team that, when it truly counted, simply couldn't summon the lineup magic or the bench production required to advance, thus consigning themselves to the bitter taste of what-ifs and the long, cold analysis of a season cut short.

Arizona Queer moves on to the Championship, a deserved ascent for a team proving its mettle when it matters most. For The Flamboyant Commish, however, the curtain has fallen, the lights are off, and the only remaining task is to pack up their gear and contemplate exactly where it all went wrong in Week 16.

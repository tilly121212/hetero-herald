The Crown Sits Uneasily: King Tastes Humble Pie

Reigning Defending King , after a regal start to the season, learned that even a crown needs to be earned every Sunday.
By Vince Malloy · Beat Writer, The Hetero Herald

And so it was, that the self-proclaimed monarch, Reigning Defending King , found its carefully constructed early-season dominion crumble in a rather un-royal fashion, yielding a 11.12-point victory to the surprisingly assertive Solid team, but I’m gay. A 3-1 record, it seems, can still be knocked off its high horse by a 2-2 challenger.

What exactly was "reigning" about a performance that coughed up just 119.88 points when 131 were needed? The "defending" part of that moniker seems less like an assertion of strength and more like a desperate plea, especially when a mere 11.12 points were the difference between continued reign and a rather undignified tumble.

This league has seen enough false prophets and premature coronations to know that a shiny record in September means precisely nothing when your roster can't deliver in October. We'll see if the King can regain its footing, or if this early stumble is a sign of a very long, un-royal road ahead.
